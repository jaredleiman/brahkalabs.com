Why We Built Brahka Labs

📅 April 16, 2025✍️ Jared Leiman⏱ 7 min read

A personal essay from Jared Leiman, founder of Brahka Labs, on the problem that drove him to start the company — and why building AI automation tools for businesses buried in paperwork is the most important work he could be doing right now.

I've Been Living This Problem for Years

Before Brahka Labs, I ran American Apostille — a document authentication company that helps individuals, law firms, businesses, and government agencies get documents certified for international use. It sounds straightforward. In practice, it's one of the most paperwork-intensive businesses you can imagine.

Every day, we were processing hundreds of documents that needed to be authenticated through a specific chain: notarization, state-level authentication, federal authentication, consulate legalization. The requirements varied by country, by document type, by intended use. The rules changed without warning. And every client had a different situation.

For years, we handled this manually. We had spreadsheets. We had checklists. We had people whose entire job was tracking documents through the authentication process. And we were still missing things, delaying shipments, spending hours on phone calls that could have been automated messages.

The paperwork wasn't just an inconvenience. It was consuming the business.

The Moment That Changed Everything

The turning point wasn't a single moment — it was a slow accumulation. Over several years, I watched talented, educated people spend their days on work that a well-designed system should be able to handle automatically. I watched clients wait for updates that should have been sent without anyone having to think about it. I watched deals stall because a document was missing or a deadline was missed.

When AI tools capable of handling real document workflows became available, I started experimenting. Not with the intention of building a company — just trying to solve the problems in front of me.

What I found was that the results were extraordinary. Tasks that had been consuming hours were being completed in minutes. Errors that had been endemic to our process disappeared. Client communication that had required constant staff attention was running on autopilot.

And I realized: this isn't a problem unique to document authentication. This is a problem every professional services business faces.

The Universal Problem We're Solving

Talk to a law firm partner. Ask an immigration attorney. Sit with the billing manager at a medical practice. Walk through the back office of a real estate firm. Everywhere you look, you find the same pattern: extraordinarily capable people spending significant portions of their time on documentation tasks that should be automated.

The problem isn't skill or intelligence. The problem is that the tools available to most businesses haven't kept pace with the complexity of their workflows.

A solo immigration attorney might spend 40% of their week on form preparation. An international real estate paralegal might spend half their time extracting data from documents and re-entering it into other systems. A medical practice billing coordinator might process 50 prior authorizations per week, each requiring 20–30 minutes of manual effort.

All of that work follows repeatable processes. All of it generates predictable outputs from predictable inputs. All of it can be automated.

The question is whether the businesses affected by this problem have access to the technology and expertise to build those automations. Most don't — not because they lack the will, but because building good automation requires specialized technical skills that most businesses can't afford to hire in-house.

Why Brahka Labs Exists

Brahka Labs exists to bridge that gap. We build custom AI automation tools for businesses that are drowning in documentation and manual workflows. We don't sell generic software. We don't ask you to adapt your processes to fit a product. We build tools that fit your specific workflow — and we do it in one week for a flat fee.

The business model is intentional. A flat fee means you know exactly what you're committing to. One week means you're not waiting months for results you're not sure you'll get. Both of those things matter enormously to the small and mid-size businesses we work with.

The industries we focus on — law, healthcare, international business, real estate — aren't arbitrary. They're the industries where the documentation burden is highest, where the stakes are the most significant, and where the gap between current state and what's possible is widest.

What We Believe About Automation

We have a few core beliefs that shape how we approach every client engagement:

  • Automation should serve people, not replace them. The goal isn't to eliminate jobs. It's to eliminate the parts of jobs that no talented person should be spending their time on. When the manual overhead is gone, people can focus on the work that actually requires their expertise.
  • Generic software can't solve specific problems. Every business has its own specific workflow, its own systems, its own edge cases. Automation that doesn't account for those specifics will fail at exactly the moments when it matters most. Custom tools built for your specific workflow are dramatically more effective than generic platforms.
  • Results should be measurable from day one. We're not selling a promise of future value. We're building tools that solve specific problems, and the impact of those tools should be measurable immediately — in time saved, in errors reduced, in revenue captured.
  • Simplicity beats sophistication. The best automation is the kind that runs silently in the background and just works. We resist the temptation to build complex systems when simple ones will do the job.

Where We're Going

The automation opportunity in professional services is enormous. We're at the beginning of a transformation in how these businesses operate — the firms and practices that adapt now will have structural advantages that compound over time. The ones that wait will find it increasingly difficult to compete with counterparts that are operating at dramatically lower administrative cost with dramatically higher accuracy.

Brahka Labs is building that transformation, one business at a time. We're not trying to be everything to everyone. We're trying to be exactly the right partner for businesses that are ready to stop letting documentation overhead define the limits of what they can accomplish.
